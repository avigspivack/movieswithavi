This was not the best movie ever, but I definitely enjoyed it, especially the fact that it didn’t rely on any modernized technology; in fact, the protagonist uses a typewriter to transcribe audio that he plays on old cassette tapes - there was something pleasing about this, but I realize it won’t be for everyone. I’m also a big fan of the lead actor here, Francois Cluzet, who is a French favorite and stars in one of my all-time favorites, “Tell No One” (and if you haven’t seen that one, GET ON IT). Cluzet does his job very well even if the role is slightly limited and the puzzle pieces (both literal and metaphorical never quite come together all the way). At a tidy 90 minutes, you won’t regret it.
