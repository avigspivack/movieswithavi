It’s clear that Louis Garrel has spent his life around movies - he seems to have found a tone and style that suits him, playful and wise. He is also kind of unfairly handsome, especially with his perfectly unkempt hair flowing in the Parisian breeze. Oh did I forget to mention that he wrote, directed, and starred in this delightful film? He did. He also blends together multiple genres - familial drama, comedy, and heist - into a seamless catchy narrative that reeks of originality. Noemie Merlant (who you may have seen as the assistant in “Tar”) is fantastic as Abel’s sidekick best friend, and their chemistry is spot on. A truly wonderful and messy love story.
