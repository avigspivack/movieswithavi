Give me a Woody Allen inspired Jewish comedy any day of the week and I am there. This one was perfect for a gray summer Saturday. Was it a miraculous film of great depth into the Jewish consciousness and a sharp, insightful commentary on the state of the world? Absolutely not. But it was damn fun, had some witty twists, a bunch of laugh out loud moments, and it was just the right length (also a nod to Woody’s typical trademark 90-minute film that I do miss). This movie won’t blow you away, but it will leave you smiling, and that’s pretty much exactly what it tries to do. Bravo.
