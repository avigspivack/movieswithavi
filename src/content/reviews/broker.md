After the excellent “Shoplifters” a few years ago, we have watched at least three more films by the wise and beautiful filmmaker, Hirokazu Kore-eda; each of them has revealed another layer of his depth, as well as his desire to explore the dynamics of family, challenging our perceptions and feelings with each turn and gorgeous shot. His newest work “Broker” begins at night with an air of mystery, and it unfolds as a road trip movie, a brilliantly ambiguous take on family and love, and leaves us wishing to be with his characters for many more hours or days.
