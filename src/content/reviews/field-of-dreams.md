Since it’s baseball season, I decided to go with a classic, and my favorite baseball movie (even though this is more of a family film, or a hero’s journey). I’ve seen this many times, so I didn’t need to rewatch it to be reminded that I cry at the end every time. And that I anticipate key moments when I know I will feel the brilliant weight of the direction - and the underrated score from the masterful James Horner - come crashing through the screen. Kevin Costner, say what you will, is probably the only guy who could play the lead role, especially at this moment in his career (1989) - he is also totally natural throwing a baseball. And even though the late Ray Liotta (as “Shoeless” Joe Jackson) is not, he, James Earl Jones, and the immortal Burt Lancaster bring their expected gravitas; only Amy Madigan feels a little overdone, but we can let that casting error slide. Ultimately, this is a story about how baseball is really an allegory for life, how it gives you second chances, can fulfill your dreams, and even soothe the soul, if you let it.
