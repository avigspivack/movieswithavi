This is a beautiful, electric, passionate, and complicated movie. I imagine it hurtles a sharp critique at conservative Georgian culture, but it also celebrates the bonds of family that are implicit. The story follows Merab, played impressively by Levan Gelbakhiani - he seems to be in every scene and the camera cannot stay away from him. He is a professional dancer as well, and it’s a must in this role. He flows and dances and seduces his way through his life with vulnerability, pride, and a fading innocence. An unexpected gem.
