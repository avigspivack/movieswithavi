I have written before about the director Christian Petzold – his growing catalog of films definitely puts him in the top tier of my favorites (“Afire” and “Transit” especially). This is his newest, and Paula Beer in the lead role (same as “Afire”) is simply sublime – I would watch her for two hours reading a phone book (yes I know they don't exist anymore). What I appreciate the most is the seeming simplicity – basically four actors in the whole movie, so much unspoken, and yet so much depth. It moves slowly and deliberately, unwinding in front of us; and as Petzold loves to do, he plays with identity – I won't say much more so as not to spoil the mystery lurking beneath the surface of the “broken” home and awkwardness that surrounds us. And that is Petzold's whole jam – nothing is quite as it seems on the surface. Each character navigating their life as best they can, grappling with grief or tragedy or finding themselves. A beautiful film.
