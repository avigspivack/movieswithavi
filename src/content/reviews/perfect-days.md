When the credits began to roll, I thought that was a perfect movie; every frame, gesture, line of dialogue, all of it was perfect. And perfectly natural. The great Wim Wenders has made a rare film here, one that transcends the mundane and encourages - almost demands - that we look at our lives through a new lens, one where we embrace a “now is now” philosophy. The pace and lack of dialogue won’t appeal to everyone, but I couldn’t recommend this movie more. They truly don’t make them like this anymore; if you can immerse yourself in it, you will be transported.
