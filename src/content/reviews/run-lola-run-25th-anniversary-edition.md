Yes, my rating is accurate. We had the pleasure of seeing the 25th anniversary version of this movie in the theater last week, and I can safely say this is one of my all-time favorites. Because one of the main qualities of a great movie’s greatness is whether its greatness holds up after many years. And what’s remarkable is how energizing and utterly modern this Tom Tykwer film is, even while watching the two main characters talk on corded telephones for much of the action. I won’t even tell you too much here other than it’s one of the most inventive and original pieces of film work I have experienced, and its themes of time and destiny are evergreen.
